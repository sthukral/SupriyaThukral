Current Ventures

Aside from my regular coursework at McMaster, at the start of September, I made it a goal to continue learning and growing outside of school work.

And, this commitment has led me to pursue some interests that challenge me on the daily.

Collaborating with Small Businesses

Since the onset of the pandemic in the March of 2020, many small businesses have had to pivot. The new predominantly virtual environment allowed me to create a series called "Friendor Friday," in which I conducted a live Instagram interview of local businesses through the summer of 2020 and part of 2021. The main goal of this series has been to highlight small businesses and their products or services in an intimate setting that shows the human side of their business. This journey has changed my life through meeting new people, gaining skills such as public speaking, acting on camera and building a virtual distribution channel - it's been very rewarding.

In 2022, I will add to this series by helping small businesses with their websites, marketing strategy, and online presence.

Transitioning to a New Industry

From 2012 - 2017, I completed my first undergraduate degree in Biology and Psychology. However, after going through the process of applying to medical schools and even interviewing at two of them, I realized that I did not want to go down that path.

Countless exams and sheer memory work were not fueling my entrepreneurial flame or adding value to my life. So I took some time to explore my options, and along the way, learned a lot about the back end of running a business full-time. I quickly became acquainted with building a website from scratch, learning how to market my photography skills, working with people from all cultures and backgrounds, and basically getting out of my comfort zone every day.

However, throughout this journey, I felt most challenged and eager to learn how I could present myself in the online world. So, in the summer of 2020, during the onset of the pandemic, I officially enrolled in my first Computer Science course, and the rest is history.

Continuous Learning!

In 2021, I made myself a promise to continue learning every day. Since September 2021, I have been the VP of Sponsorships for DeltaHacks, McMaster's own student-led hackathon. I have had the opportunity to learn plenty from my peers, and I've networked with some amazing tech-based companies.

While I observed several hackathons during the year, I finally submitted my first-ever hackathon project at HackWestern and gave my first-ever pitch. Although I was exhausted from the long weekend of hacking, all the learning was well worth it.
